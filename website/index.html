A Declining Bee Population

Major pollinators are facing a reduced population globally!

As a result of many human-driven factors, the bees may face extinction.

Honey bees — wild and domestic — perform about 80 percent of all pollination worldwide. A single bee colony can pollinate 300 million flowers each day. Grains are primarily pollinated by the wind, but fruits, nuts and vegetables are pollinated by bees. Seventy out of the top 100 human food crops — which supply about 90 percent of the world’s nutrition — are pollinated by bees. The key issue this website will be covering is the reduction of the population in the United States from climate change, pesticide use, and habitat loss.

Consequences of Pollinator Loss

Believe it or not, you have a bee to thank for every one in three bites of food you eat.

The first half of the site is dedicated to the environmental harm caused by pesticide use, with particular focus given to the impacts on biodiversity, soil health, genetics and the water system. And though these adverse impacts are commonly felt across a wide geography, this site examines how their immediate ecological impacts are often in predominately lower-income, minority communities.
