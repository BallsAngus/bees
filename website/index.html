A Declining Bee Population

We are currently facing a global issue regarding the decline of pollinators. What is causing it, and what does it mean for the future?

Julian Tuazon, ESPM 50AC, Spring 2022

The research of this website, broadly speaking, focuses on the negative externalities of pesticide use. In particular, specific attention is given to the often detrimental environmental and health impacts that result from the persistent dispersion of pesticides in commercial agriculture. Moreover, this site also highlights the unfortunate fact that the groups who primarily experience these consequences are often those with less economic means and who regularly face various forms of discrimination in American society. And finally, though not exclusively focused on California, this site attempts to bring light to problems that are particularly pertinent to California and often uses research on areas in the state itself.

The first half of the site is dedicated to the environmental harm caused by pesticide use, with particular focus given to the impacts on biodiversity, soil health, genetics and the water system. And though these adverse impacts are commonly felt across a wide geography, this site examines how their immediate ecological impacts are often in predominately lower-income, minority communities.

The second part of the site discusses the effects of pesticide use on human health. Particularly, it covers research on the health impacts of those who work in, or live near, fields where pesticides are used. Additionally, part of this section is dedicated to the disproportionate impact of those who face health concerns as a result of proximity to pesticide use, on a racial and financial basis.
